# BCPL Supply Chain Cost Leakage — Insight Report
**Bongo Consumer Products Ltd. | January–December 2025**

---

## The Story in One Line

BCPL lost ৳29.22 lakh in hidden supply chain costs in 2025 —
and 88% of it came from just two sources: overpriced import
suppliers and dead stock sitting in regional warehouses.

### 📦 Procurement — The Biggest Culprit

3 import suppliers consistently charged above contract prices:

- **MegaChem Corp** overcharged by 18.75% on average → ৳4.03 lakh lost
- **GlobalPack Imports** overcharged by 16.80% → ৳2.87 lakh lost
- **AsiaFood Commodities** overcharged by 11.51% → ৳2.40 lakh lost

These 3 suppliers = 20% of BCPL's supplier base,
yet they drove **59% of total procurement leakage.**

Making it worse: emergency orders carried a **20.8% price variance**
— more than 6x the regular order rate of 3.3%.
Every unplanned purchase is costing BCPL significantly more.

---

### 🏭 Inventory — Dead Stock is Draining Cash

23.3% of BCPL's inventory snapshots were flagged as dead stock
(unsold for 90+ days). Average dead stock age: **126 days.**

**Where it's worst:**
- Sylhet Regional Depot → ৳3.61 lakh in dead stock holding cost
- Chattogram Regional Hub → ৳3.00 lakh
- Khulna Distribution Center → ৳2.69 lakh

**What's sitting there:**
- Packaged Food drives **49.88%** of dead stock leakage
  (avg age 132 days — expiry risk is real)
- Personal Care: 35.63% of dead stock leakage

Sylhet's rural distribution network is causing stock to pile up
far beyond demand — and BCPL is paying to store products
that aren't moving.

---

### 🚚 Logistics — Two Routes Are Bleeding Money

**Sylhet Tea Belt:**
- 40% of shipments arrive late
- 32.83% average freight cost overrun
- Total leakage: ৳61,792

**Khulna–Jessore Belt:**
- 34.9% late delivery rate
- Highest penalty cost: ৳28,500
- Total leakage: ৳57,429

**Dhaka–Sylhet Highway** had the highest absolute freight overrun
(৳97,439) due to long distance — but its late rate (19.2%) is
more manageable than the above two routes.

Meanwhile, **Dhaka Metro and Dhaka–Chattogram Highway**
performed cleanly — proving the problem is route-specific,
not company-wide.

---

### 📋 Fulfillment — Small but Visible

10.7% of orders had errors (returns or partial shipments).
Partial shipments (৳52,800) cost more than returns (৳14,400).
Khulna warehouse had the highest error rate.

At 2.25% of total leakage, fulfillment is the lowest priority —
but the pattern points to stock inconsistency at Khulna,
which connects back to the inventory problem.
